Pygmy Fruit Bat

Aethalops alecto

Classification

Phylum: Arthropoda
Class: Mammalia
Order: Chiroptera
Family: Pteropodidae
Genus: Aethalops
Species: alecto

Description

Forelimb length: 4.65 cm
Mass: 15 g
Coloration: brown or black fur

Habitat

Native to: Southeast Asia; i.e., Malaysia, Indonesia, et cetera
Found in: forests on mountains, ostensibly not found at elevations lower than 1 km

Feeding

As the name implies, it is an herbivorous bat. It uses sight and smell to locate food, tearing fruit off trees with their teeth before flying to their roosts to actually eat. It also helps disperse seeds by doing this.

It has been posited that during light rainfall, they may straighten their hind legs, forming an upside-down "V" shape, and lick water as it reaches the head.

Digestion

A. alecto has a fairly comman digestive tract including an esophagus, stomach, et cetera. It has more enzymes that process sugar than insectivorous bats due to its diet.

Excretion

Waste products are discharged as guano.

Respiration

A. alecto shares a system similar to other mammals, with lungs, a heart, hemoglobin (oxygen-carrying blood cells) and et cetera; any difference comes from a greater efficiency due to having evolved to fly. However, it still primarily possesses mammalian lungs, and thus cannot fly as efficiently as birds (since air must travel bidirectionally through them).

Circulation

This works similarly to other mammals as well, with a heart to direct blood flow through arteries and arterioles, and capillaries, and back through venules and veins. Oxygen is, of course, exchanged in the lungs via a separate circuit.

Reproduction

Sperm is passed through a male's penis into a female's vagina. The breeding months are from February to June, and the gestation period is roughly 4-6 months, and generally only one bat is born per litter. The male does not do very much to assist in nurturing the offspring.

Trivia

  • Along with other fruit bats, pygmy fruit bats do not use echolocation, as insectivorous bats do.
  • Its species name, alecto, is the name of one of the Erinyes, or Furies, in Greek mythology, meaning "unceasing anger". (Interesting name for an herbivorous bat.)
  • It had controversially been considered the same species as the only other of the genus, A. aequalis (which was recorded later), though the two are currently considered separate species.
