Scarlet Pygmy

Nannophya pygmaea

"Adult Male."
Retrieved 14 Jun 2019 from https://sgodonata.files.wordpress.com/2015/09/img_9630-2015-9-5.png. Author: Leonard Tan

Classification

Phylum: Arthropoda
Class: Insecta
Order: Odonata
Family: Libellulidae
Genus: Nannophya
Species: pygmaea

Description

Wingspan (hindwing): 12-13 mm
Body length: 16-17 mm
Coloration:
  • Males - red body; red-black eyes; clear wings. The wing bases have a yellow tint
  • Females - striped brown-white body; brown-white eyes. Wing tint is darker than that of males.

Habitat

Native to: Australia and various Asiatic countries including China, Korea, Indonesia, Japan, and Taiwan
Found in: areas with standing water, such as marshes and abandoned patty fields

Feeding

The scarlet pygmy flies around and catches any prey via its legs (all positioned towards the anterior) and mouth.

Digestion

Dragonflies like the scarlet pygmy have full digestive tracts with an esophagus, stomach, intestines, et cetera; they also have a crop in the esophagus, where they can store food if the stomach is already busy digesting food.

Excretion

The scarlet pygmy, like other dragonflies, excrete any waste products through the end of their digestive tracts, i.e., the anus.

Respiration

Oxygen enters the body via pores in the abdomen; these pores are known as spiracles. From the spiracles, air is distributed to nearby cells. This is part of the reason dragonflies and other insects are limited in their ability to grow; the lack of a central respiratory system prevents them from being able to sustain a significantly larger body.

Circulation

The scarlet pygmy and other insects have hemolymph, a fluid that flows freely through the body. There is a dorsal vessel, which includes a heart, that helps maintain hemolymph flow to the head; accessory pumps manage its flow through the dragonfly's wings.

Reproduction

Males and females mate in mid-air; the male carries the female at least partially, allowing the female to conserve energy for egg laying. The female lays eggs on a substrate subsequently.

Trivia

  • The scarlet pygmy is the smallest known extant dragonfly.
  • It, and other dragonflies, can propel themselves by shooting water from the anus.
  • Both its genus and species name refer to its small size; "Nanno" in the genus name (as in "nano") and "pygmaea" (meaning pygmy, or dwarf) in the species name.
